Molecular Simulation

All molecular simulations were performed with the openmm software within its python API. The trajectory that you can see below were simulated under Langevin dynamics at a temperature 300K, non periodic conditions with cutoff rc = 1nm, a friction coefficient equal 1 ps-1 , no bond constraints and a timestep equal 1 fs. This configuration is obtained after 25000 steps of energy minimization.
At the end of the simulation with openmm, we store the positions of the 22 atoms that constitutes the alanine dipeptide molecule for 2500 times.
We generate the movie with VMD and VideoMatch softwares.

Openmm and VMD

OpenMM is a toolkit for molecular simulation. It can be used either as a stand-alone application for running simulations, or as a library you call from your own code. It provides a combination of extreme flexibility (through custom forces and integrators), openness, and high performance (especially on recent GPUs) that make it truly unique among simulation codes. Visual Molecular Dynamics (VMD) is a molecular modelling and visualization computer program. VMD is developed as mainly a tool to view and analyze the results of molecular dynamics simulations. It also includes tools for working with volumetric data, sequence data, and arbitrary graphics objects.
